Optical LAN Training: Operations, Provisioning & Maintenance and Advanced Troubleshooting

Do you operate a mission critical highly secure, high availability Tellabs Optical LAN?

This course is designed for Network Operations and/or any individuals responsible for operating, provisioning, maintaining and monitoring a Tellabs Optical LAN system. This course is also intended for employees responsible for the administration, installation, testing, maintenance or technical support of the Optical LAN.

Prerequisites

Basic knowledge in Ethernet switching and routing; including VLAN concepts, IP/subnetting, basic QoS knowledge and the basic concepts of port security. Target Audience

Class Specifics

Course Duration: 4.5 days

Class Location: Public Classroom Offering in Dallas, TX or Private On-Site

Lab Activities: 80% Hands On; 2:1 student-to-equipment ratio

Class Size: 4-8 Students

Course Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Identify the features and functionality of the Tellabs Optical LAN Access Solution
  2. Describe the network functionality of the Tellabs Optical LAN Access Solution
  3. Describe the common equipment associated with the Tellabs Optical LAN Access Solution
  4. Perform provisioning for the Tellabs Optical LAN voice and data services using the Tellabs Panorama PON Manager
  5. Perform equipment management and monitoring using the Tellabs Panorama PON Manager
  6. Perform trouble clearing procedures of common problems
  7. Understand PON fiber optic topologies and communication processes
  8. Understand the terms, jargon, and measurements used in PON fiber optics
  9. Perform PON fiber optic testing and troubleshooting using modern techniques and tools.
  10. Understand and practice the standard guidelines for PON fiber optic installation, safety, and documentation
